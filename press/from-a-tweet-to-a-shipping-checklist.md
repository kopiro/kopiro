# I installed Fedora on an old Dell Latitude, and Linux laptop UX has changed a lot

Inspired by [@antirez](https://x.com/antirez), I picked up an old plastic Dell Latitude and installed Fedora.

I never seriously used Linux on a laptop in the last years. My only real Linux period was when I was a teenager trying hard to move away from Windows. Then I switched to macOS and stayed there.

After a few days using Fedora as a daily machine, these are my honest notes.

## 1) Fedora UI and polish: genuinely impressive

I had massively overestimated the "macOS gap".

Fedora feels polished, coherent, and much closer to a premium desktop experience than I expected. Linux desktop UX has clearly caught up a lot while I was not paying attention.

## 2) Developer experience: on par (sometimes better)

For my day-to-day work, the developer experience is basically on par with macOS.

In some areas it even feels better:

- tooling is immediate
- package and CLI workflows feel natural
- the stuff I already use is mostly *nix-first anyway

At the end of the day, most of my macOS setup was already built around unix tools. So the transition was much smoother than expected.

## 3) The one real pain: the touchpad

The only serious issue for me is the touchpad.

Oh my god.

MacBook touchpads are so good that I forgot how frustrating trackpads can be on other systems. From what I can tell, this is a mix of weaker hardware and less refined software behavior.

This is the only area where I immediately miss macOS every single minute.

## Final thought

Linux on laptops in 2026 is not what I remembered.

If your mental model is still based on "it works but it's rough", it may be outdated. Mine definitely was.
